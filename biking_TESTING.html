Biking in NYC

October 2023

Data Visualization

This was a project for Information Design, a course taught by Katherine Dillon.

The project is a set of data visualizations aiming to answer the question - how safe is it to bike in NYC? These visualizations were made using publicly available data sets about biking in New York City, revealing information on Bicycle Counts, Bike Lane Amounts, Biking Distribution, and Injury and Fatality Counts throughout the past decade.

The New York City Department of Transportation and the New York City Department of Planning made the data sets available.

The project explores the current state of biking in NYC by analyzing trends and visualizing key data points. This includes showing how bike lane expansion correlates with increased bike safety and usage.

In conclusion, biking in NYC has grown significantly and shows promise as a sustainable and safe mode of transport. With data-backed insights, the project highlights the positive impact of dedicated bike lanes and public engagement with cycling.
